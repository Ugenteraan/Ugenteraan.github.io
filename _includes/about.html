About

As an integral component of my pursuit towards attaining a master's degree, I have had the honor of publishing two papers. The primary focus of both papers revolved around examining the efficacy of Capsule Networks in practical applications. The title of the first paper was "CapsNet vs CNN: Analysis Of The Effects Of Varying Feature Spatial Arrangement." The second paper, titled "Survey on Capsule Networks Depth Scalability and Learned Feature Spatial Relationships Retention," delved into an exploration of the depth scalability of Capsule Networks and their ability to retain learned feature spatial relationships. Should you wish to access these papers, they are available for download through the respective links provided below.

I adopt a methodical approach to acquiring proficiency in machine learning algorithms. This entails deriving the algorithms mathematically and implementing them with minimal reliance on external libraries. Some of my works are Kalman Filter, Principal Component Analysis, Naive Bayes, Fourier Transform, Neural Network, and Eigenface.
